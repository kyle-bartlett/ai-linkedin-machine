Thanks for sharing the summary of **"Attention-based feature Online Conformal Prediction (AFOCP)"**! It appears to be a significant advancement in online conformal prediction, particularly for time series and non-stationary data.

To highlight the key takeaways:

- **Why feature space calibration matters:**  
  Traditional OCP uses nonconformity measures directly on model outputs, which might be noisy or affected by irrelevant variation. AFOCP leverages deep neural network embeddings, which distill task-relevant info and suppress nuisances, thereby making conformal scores more informative and resulting in tighter prediction sets.

- **Role of the attention-based weighting:**  
  Instead of treating all historical data points equally or using fixed decay schemes, AFOCP dynamically assesses which past points are more relevant to the current input via attention. This adaptivity crucially helps in settings with shifts or temporal dependencies, where past data relevance may vary.

- **Theoretical guarantees:**  
  Maintaining rigorous coverage guarantees in an online, potentially adversarial setting is non-trivial. The paper proves that despite the feature-space calibration and adaptive weighting, coverage is preserved and intervals are tighter under reasonable assumptions.

- **Practical impact:**  
  The strong empirical results — up to 88% reduction in prediction set size without sacrificing coverage — imply that AFOCP can make conformal prediction more practical in real-world streaming and non-stationary applications.
