### Overview of **SDMVC**:

**Problem Addressed:**
- In multi-view clustering, each data view (e.g., different feature sets or modalities) contributes to the overall clustering task.
- Some views may have unclear or poor clustering structures, which can negatively impact the final clustering results if naively combined.

**Key Idea:**
- Learn view-specific embedded features independently using deep autoencoders to capture unique characteristics without interference.
- Concatenate these learned features into a global representation that diminishes the adverse effects of noisy or uninformative views.
- Use a self-supervised mechanism to generate pseudo-labels and a unified target distribution.
- Guide all views to collaboratively learn discriminative features via this unified target, improving clustering consistency while preserving diversity among views.

---

### Strengths and Novelty:

- **Handling Noisy Views:** Unlike many prior works that assume all views equally contribute, SDMVC explicitly addresses the problem of “bad views.”
- **Self-Supervised Pseudo-Labeling:** Innovatively employs pseudo-labels to unify learning targets and supervise feature embedding improvements without external labels.
- **View Independence and Fusion:** Balances the need for each view to retain its unique information and the benefits of multi-view fusion.
- **Performance:** Demonstrated superior clustering results on multiple datasets over 14 baseline methods, indicating robustness and effectiveness.
- **Code Availability:** The authors provide open-source code, allowing reproducibility and potential practical application or extension.
