Using GPT-4 to analyze qualitative data is indeed a powerful approach that can transform how we handle and interpret large volumes of text-based data. GPT-4's advanced natural language understanding allows it to identify themes, sentiments, patterns, and insights from qualitative data faster and often more accurately than traditional manual methods.

Here are some viable uses of GPT-4 for qualitative data analysis:

1. **Thematic Analysis**  
   GPT-4 can categorize large datasets (e.g., interview transcripts, open-ended survey responses) into themes or topics automatically, helping researchers identify recurring ideas without reading every word manually.

2. **Sentiment Analysis**  
   It can detect emotions and attitudes expressed in the data, providing nuanced insights into user opinions, customer feedback, or social media discussions.

3. **Summarization**  
   GPT-4 can generate concise summaries of lengthy texts, extracting key points and main ideas, which aids in quick decision-making.

4. **Coding and Tagging**  
   It can assign codes or labels to qualitative data units (sentences, paragraphs) consistently, facilitating organization and retrieval of information.

5. **Comparative Analysis**  
   GPT-4 can compare differing perspectives or responses across demographic groups or time periods to reveal changes, contrasts, or consensus.

6. **Anomaly or Outlier Detection**  
   By flagging unusual or contradictory responses, GPT-4 can help research teams focus on interesting cases or errors in the data.

7. **Automated Reporting**  
   GPT-4 can draft reports and interpret findings with contextual explanations, saving time in writing and enhancing stakeholder communication.
