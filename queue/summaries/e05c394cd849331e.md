### Key Objectives
- **Advance AI safety research:** Support or coordinate research efforts, potentially through grants, workshops, or partnerships.
- **Identify and promote best practices and standards:** Develop or endorse guidelines around development, testing, transparency, and deployment.
- **Facilitate information sharing:** Create forums, conferences, or online platforms for stakeholders to exchange knowledge.
- **Policy engagement:** Serve as a liaison between technical experts and policymakers to inform regulation and public understanding.

### Organizational Structure
- Establish a **diverse advisory board** including AI researchers, ethicists, industry leaders, and government representatives.
- Consider forming committees focused on research, standards, outreach, and policy.

### Collaboration and Partnerships
- Partner with existing organizations such as **Partnership on AI, IEEE, OECD, and relevant academic institutions** to leverage expertise and avoid duplication.
- Foster international collaboration to address the global nature of AI safety.

### Transparency and Accountability
- Commit to transparency in activities and decision-making processes.
- Consider mechanisms for **accountability** to the public and stakeholders.

### Activities and Deliverables
- Organize regular **conferences, workshops, or webinars** on frontier AI safety challenges.
- Publish **white papers, guidelines, and reports** on best practices and developments.
- Develop **toolkits or resources** for companies to implement safety measures.
  
### Funding and Sustainability
- Identify diverse funding sources: membership fees, grants, sponsorships from industry.
- Ensure independence to maintain credibility regarding safety and ethical issues.

### Communication and Outreach
- Maintain an accessible website and regular communications (newsletters, social media).
- Engage with media to promote public awareness about AI safety.
