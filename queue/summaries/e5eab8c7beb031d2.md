Condé Nast is a mass media company known primarily for publishing some of the world's most prestigious magazines. Founded in 1909 by Condé Montrose Nast, the company has built a strong reputation in the magazine publishing industry.

### Key Facts about Condé Nast:

- **Industry:** Media & Publishing
- **Founded:** 1909 by Condé Montrose Nast
- **Headquarters:** One World Trade Center, New York City, USA
- **Notable Publications:** 
  - Vogue
  - The New Yorker
  - Vanity Fair
  - GQ
  - Wired
  - Architectural Digest
  - Bon Appétit
  - Teen Vogue
- **Business Focus:** Primarily print and digital media including magazines, websites, video content, and events.
- **Parent Company:** As of 2019, Condé Nast is a subsidiary of Advance Publications, which is privately owned by the Newhouse family.

Condé Nast has evolved from a traditional magazine publisher into a digital media company, with strong online presences for many of its brands and increased focus on video content and social media engagement. The company is recognized globally for its influence on fashion, culture, lifestyle, and journalism.
